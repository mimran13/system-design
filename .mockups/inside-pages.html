CAP Theorem

When a network partition happens, a distributed system can keep serving reads and writes or keep them consistent — not both. CAP is the constraint that shapes every distributed-data choice you'll make.

⏱ 7 min read 📅 Updated Jun 2026 🎯 interview-critical
interview-criticalfor-scale
👁 You'll see this when…
  • Your multi-region database returns stale reads right after a failover.
  • A network blip splits the cluster and half your writes start failing.
  • Someone asks "is this CP or AP?" in a design review and the room goes quiet.
  • Your queue says "delivered" but the downstream never saw the message.

How it works

CAP names three properties — Consistency, Availability, Partition tolerance — and proves you can guarantee at most two. Since partitions are not optional on real networks, the real choice is C vs A during a partition.

⤢⛶
Client write
→
Node A
⇿
⚡ partition
⇿
Node B
During the partition: reject the write (CP) or accept it and reconcile later (AP).

When to choose CP vs AP

ChooseWhenExamples
CP — consistencyStale or conflicting reads cause real harm (money, inventory, locks)etcd, ZooKeeper, Spanner
AP — availabilityServing something beats serving nothing; conflicts can be mergedCassandra, DynamoDB, Riak

Anti-patterns

Anti-patternWhy it hurtsBetter
"We're CA, we don't partition"Partitions are physics, not a config flagDecide C-vs-A explicitly per dataset
One CAP choice for the whole systemBilling and analytics have different needsPer-service / per-table choice
💡 What interviewers are testing

That you know CAP is about behaviour during a partition, not an everyday three-way trade-off.

Strong answer pattern:

  1. State that P is non-negotiable on a real network.
  2. Frame the choice as C vs A during the partition.
  3. Tie it to the data: money → CP, feed → AP.
  4. Mention PACELC for the else-latency case.

Test yourself

Why is "CA" not a real operating choice?
Because partitions will happen regardless. When one does, a "CA" system must drop either C or A anyway — so it was really CP or AP all along.
Your payments ledger: CP or AP, and why?
CP. A double-spend or lost debit from a stale read is unacceptable; rejecting writes during a partition is the safer failure mode.
What does PACELC add to CAP?
Else-Latency-Consistency: even with no partition, you still trade latency against consistency. CAP only covers the partition case.
